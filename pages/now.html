navanirmāṇa · नवनिर्माण

a city reborn

The Ayodhya of today is undergoing the most profound transformation in its millennia-long history. With the construction of the magnificent Rāma Mandir and the redevelopment of the pilgrimage corridor, the city is being reshaped to accommodate millions of visitors while preserving its sacred character. Wide boulevards, modern amenities, and restored water bodies now complement the ancient temples.

The government's Ayodhya Development Plan envisions a world-class heritage city. Over ₹30,000 crore has been invested in infrastructure, including a new international airport, railway station, and riverfront promenade. Yet the planners have consciously retained the spiritual essence—the narrow lanes, the bustling bazaars, and the timeless rhythm of temple bells.

punarujjīvana · पुनरुज्जीवन

heritage & contemporary life

Modern Ayodhya is not just about concrete and steel. There is a conscious effort to revive traditional arts, crafts, and performing traditions that had declined. The Ayodhya Cultural Centre now hosts regular Kathak performances, Ramlila festivals, and craft fairs. Local artisans are being trained in traditional stone carving, painting, and textile work to sustain livelihoods.

The Ram Katha Park hosts daily storytelling sessions, attracting scholars and tourists. Digital museums and interpretation centres have been set up to educate visitors about the city's layered history. The city is also becoming a hub for spiritual tourism, with yoga and meditation centres mushrooming along the river.

bhaviṣya · भविष्य

the road ahead

Ayodhya's transformation is an ongoing journey. The coming years will see even more ambitious projects that aim to balance heritage with modernity.

Vedic Eco Park

A 100-acre forest park along the Sarayu, recreating the hermitage setting of ancient rishis. Planned for 2026.

Ramayana Research Institute

A world-class centre for Ramayana studies, with a manuscript library and digital archive.

Heritage Hotels

Restoration of old havelis into boutique hotels, offering authentic Ayodhya experiences.

Sarayu Cruise Tourism

Electric boats for river cruises connecting major ghats, with onboard cultural performances.

Virtual Heritage Centre

AR/VR experiences recreating ancient Ayodhya, allowing visitors to 'walk' through Valmiki's city.

Green Corridor

Pedestrian-only zones with electric buggies, reducing pollution in the core heritage area.
