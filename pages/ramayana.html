vālmīki · रामायण

the story of rāma

Ayodhya’s soul-epic: exile, devotion, and the return of dharma.

bālakāṇḍa ayodhyākāṇḍa araṇyakāṇḍa·kiṣkindhā sundara·yuddha uttarakāṇḍa
bālakāṇḍa

the birth & youth

The Rāmāyaṇa opens in Ayodhya, the splendid capital of Kosala. King Daśaratha, blessed with four sons — Rāma, Bharata, Lakṣmaṇa, and Śatrughna — after performing the putreṣṭi yajña. The boys grow up in the royal palace, tutored by sages Vasiṣṭha and Viśvāmitra. Rāma’s first adventure takes him to Mithila, where he breaks the bow of Śiva and wins Sītā’s hand.

The city of Ayodhya is described in loving detail: streets perfumed with sandal, palaces studded with gems, and citizens devoted to dharma. This kāṇḍa establishes the divine lineage and the perfect world that will soon be disrupted.

ततो यज्ञे समाप्ते तु ऋत्विग्भिरभिपूजितम् । ददौ पुत्रान् दशरथो राजा देवव्रतानिव ॥
— वाल्मीकि रामायण (बालकाण्ड १८.५२)
ayodhyākāṇḍa

the departure & the vow

The coronation of Rāma is announced, and Ayodhya decks itself in celebration. But Queen Kaikeyī, manipulated by Mantharā, demands two boons from Daśaratha: her son Bharata should be crowned, and Rāma exiled for fourteen years. Rāma accepts without a flicker, Sītā and Lakṣmaṇa insist on accompanying him. The city falls into grief as the trio depart for the forest.

Daśaratha dies of a broken heart. Bharata, returning from his uncle’s kingdom, refuses the throne and places Rāma’s sandals on the throne as a symbol of his rule. He settles in Nandigrāma, awaiting Rāma’s return.

araṇyakāṇḍa · kiṣkindhā

forest & alliance

encounter with Vīradha

In the Daṇḍaka forest, the trio meets sages and slays demons. The atmosphere is one of asceticism and vigilance.

the golden deer

Mārīca, disguised as a golden deer, lures Rāma away. Rāvaṇa abducts Sītā after Jatāyu’s futile resistance.

kishkindhā

Rāma meets Hanumān and Sugrīva. He helps Sugrīva regain his kingdom, and in return Sugrīva sends monkeys to search for Sītā.

sundara · yuddha

the leap & the war

uttarakāṇḍa

the coronation

Fourteen years of exile end. Rāma, Sītā, and Lakṣmaṇa return to Ayodhya. The city is decked with flowers, and Bharata places Rāma’s sandals at his feet. The coronation ushers in the Rāma Rājya — a reign of perfect justice, peace, and prosperity. Every year, Ayodhya celebrates this homecoming as Dīpāvalī, the festival of lights.

The Rāmāyaṇa closes with the establishment of the solar dynasty in glory, and the promise that as long as the mountains stand and rivers flow, the story of Rāma will be told.

“रामो विग्रहवान् धर्मः साधुः सत्यपराक्रमः । राजा सर्वस्य लोकस्य देवानामिव वासवः”
— Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa (Yuddhakāṇḍa)
