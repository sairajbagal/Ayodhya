abodes of the sacred

Ayodhya is considered one of the seven most sacred cities (Sapta Puri) in Hinduism, capable of granting liberation (moksha). Its very soil is believed to be charged with the presence of Rama and the ancient rishis. For centuries, saints and mystics have made it their home, and countless shrines dot the cityscape—each with its own legend and spiritual energy.

The sacred geography extends beyond temples: the Sarayu river is itself a goddess, and bathing in her waters during solar eclipses or Kartik Purnima is considered supremely meritorious. The city's sacred aura attracts pilgrims not only from India but from Southeast Asia, where the Ramayana tradition remains vibrant.

upāsanā · उपासना

daily rhythms of devotion

Mangala Aarti

The first worship before dawn, when deities are awakened with lamps, conch shells, and Vedic chants. Performed at major temples.

Sarayu Aarti

Every evening at Ram ki Paidi, priests offer lamps to the river goddess. Hundreds of floating diyas create a mesmerizing sight.

Abhisheka

Ritual bathing of idols with milk, honey, and sacred water. Performed especially on Mondays and during festivals.

yātrā · पंचकोसी

the sacred circuit

Pañchkośī Yātrā

The ancient pilgrimage circuit around Ayodhya, covering approximately 45 kilometers. Devotees walk this path over five days, visiting 108 sacred spots including temples, tanks, and forests associated with Rama's legend. It is believed to cleanse all sins and grant the same merit as a horse sacrifice.

1 Day 1: Ayodhya to Saryu
2 Day 2: Saryu to Jogiana
3 Day 3: Jogiana to Ramghat
4 Day 4: Ramghat to Gopratara
5 Day 5: Return to Ayodhya
utsava · पर्व

when the city becomes a temple

Sacred time in Ayodhya is marked by festivals that transform the city into a living temple. Ram Navami (March-April) celebrates Rama's birth with processions, chariots, and mass chanting. Kartik Purnima (November) sees the ghats crowded with pilgrims taking holy dips.

Shravan Jhoola (July-August) features swings decorated with flowers, carrying idols of Rama and Sita. Ekadashi days draw thousands for fasting and vigil. Each festival is an opportunity to renew one's connection with the divine.
